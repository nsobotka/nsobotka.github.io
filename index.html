Independent Project - Phylogenetic Trees

"Reconstruction of Phylogenetic Trees via Levenshtein Distances of RNA"

Study conducted to the evaluate the effectiveness of using the edit distance (Levenshtein Distance) between RNA sequences of species in reconstructing their phylogenetic tree.

Technologies used: Python, Tkinter, NumPy

Presented at: DVSF, MontCo, PJAS (Regional and State) Science Fairs

Kod*Lab - AI Researcher (2021 - Current)

At Kod*Lab (part of GRASP Lab), I work on the Adversarial Ground project under Prof. Dan Koditschek with the goal of designing a measure for robot locomotion controller robustness using generative adversarial networks (GANs).

My focus on the team is network architecture, and more broadly ensuring that the model correctly differentiates between different surface types, and different jumpability qualities.

Primarily utilized Python, Tensorflow, and SciKit-Learn.

CHOP - Medical Software Developer (2019-2020)

As a medical software developer at the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia, I worked under Prof. Ilia Fishbein and Dr. Robert Levy to design and implement software to automate the morphometry of histologically stained, stent-implanted arteries.

In addition, I researched the application of unsupervised learning to the segmentation of medical images with the aim of maximizing automation while maintaining a high degree of accuracy.

Primarily utilized Python, PyTorch, OpenCV, and Tkinter.
